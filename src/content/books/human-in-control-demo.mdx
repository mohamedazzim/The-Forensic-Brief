## About This Book

"Human in Control" challenges the foundational assumption of modern AI governance: that keeping humans in the loop is sufficient to prevent AI failures. Through analysis of real incidents, academic research, and governance frameworks, Dr. Prakasam demonstrates that human-in-the-loop as commonly implemented is a compliance exercise, not a safety mechanism.

## Who Should Read This

- AI governance professionals building oversight systems
- Product managers designing human-AI collaboration workflows
- Regulators evaluating human oversight requirements
- CTOs and CISOs responsible for AI risk management

## Key Takeaways

- Human-in-the-loop reduces accountability without reducing risk
- Automation bias makes human oversight worse, not better
- Effective oversight requires architectural separation, not just review steps
- Accountability must be assigned to individuals, not distributed across teams
